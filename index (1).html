The Poetry Grimoire

✧
— I. FAMILIAR —
She does not fetch, nor heel, nor stay —
my familiar keeps her own counsel,
sharpens her claws on my verbs,
and sleeps, coiled, inside
the unfinished sentence
I cannot bring myself to end.
— II. CAULDRON —
Into the pot: three syllables
stolen from a sleeping god,
the colour of the hour
before dawn admits its name,
and one remembered grief
pressed like a violet between pages
of a book you'll never finish.
— III. THE WRITER —
I am not the story.
I am the space
the story falls into —
the held breath
between the last word
and the page turning.

Even silence
is a kind of grammar.
